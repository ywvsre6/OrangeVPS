## OrangeVPS Reviews

The hosting community has been weighing in on OrangeVPS since their 2023 launch, and the feedback paints an interesting picture of a young provider finding its footing.

**What Users Are Saying:**

The most consistent praise goes to their control panel. One detailed review from LowEndTalk put it this way: the panel is "one of the most comfortable ones" they've experienced. Installing from ISO just works, and the VNC setup handles keyboard layouts automatically—no more hunting for the colon key when you're in vi. That might sound like a small thing, but anyone who's fought with a clunky VPS panel knows it's the little frustrations that add up.

Performance-wise, the Kansas City location shines for US connectivity. Benchmark results show consistent speeds within North America, with one reviewer noting it achieved 900+ Mbps to US nodes with ping times between 28-41ms. The Singapore location delivers solid performance for Southeast Asian users, while the Hong Kong location offers decent latency to China Mainland (90-110ms) after their move to Equinix HK1.

**The Honest Assessment:**

For storage performance, users report impressive NVMe speeds—one test showed 4K operations hitting 42,000 IOPS and large file transfers exceeding 4GB/s. Memory speeds came in around 17,500 MB/s for reads, which is notably faster than many budget competitors.

Support response time sits around three hours on average, which is pretty reasonable for a small team. Users report that tickets get answered within a reasonable timeframe, and the VPS runs as expected for the price point.

**Real Talk on Trade-offs:**

The no-refund policy makes some users nervous, especially students and those in developing countries making budget-conscious decisions. One Indonesian review noted the biggest pain point: no local bank transfer support, only credit card payments, which isn't ideal for everyone.

For Minecraft server hosting specifically, the 2.2GHz clock speed might be limiting for larger servers. The bandwidth to Indonesia is capped at around 200Mbps, and transfer data is limited based on your plan rather than unmetered.

**Community Verdict:**

Independent reviewers conclude it's a decent product at its price point. One comprehensive benchmark noted, "this orangevps is a really decent product... I recommend it if you care most or only about connectivity within the US." The provider smartly chose good datacenter locations with solid backbone connectivity, even if global routing isn't always perfect (which, to be fair, is often beyond any single provider's control).

The general consensus? If you need affordable VPS hosting in their available locations and value a well-designed control panel, OrangeVPS delivers solid value. Just go in knowing what you're getting—honest specs, reasonable support, and infrastructure choices that prioritize stability over cutting-edge hardware.
